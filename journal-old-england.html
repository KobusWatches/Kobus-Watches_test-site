Notes on the Old England building

On the Old England building in Brussels, and how Art Nouveau influences Kobus's design process, not just its look.

The Old England building was completed in 1899 as a department store, designed by Paul Saintenoy. Its black iron and glass facade still stands a few streets from the Grand-Place, and today it houses the Musée des Instruments de Musique.

What makes it worth studying is not the decoration — it is that the ornament and the structure are the same thing. The ironwork that curls across the facade is also what holds it up. Nothing is applied afterwards.

That is the discipline Kobus borrows. A curve on the case, a line on the dial: each has to earn its place structurally, not decoratively. Art Nouveau shapes the process — the questions asked of every part — before it shapes the look.
